Types Of Car Service

Full Car Service

A full car service is ideal for an annual maintenance programme for your car and recommended every 15,000 kilometres or 12 months – Whichever is sooner. Some manufacturers may recommend more frequent checks.

Interim Car Service

Interim Servicing is ideal for vehicles used for short, mainly urban journeys or for those who wish to service vehicles doing very high mileage more regularly. An interim service every 7,500 kilometres or 6 months helps keep your car safe and road worthy between full services.

Car Service FAQs

Your car will be inspected and serviced according to our itemised service schedule using only standard or quality original parts. Each item will be thoroughly checked by our qualified mechanics and if our inspection identifies advisory work, we’ll contact you for further instructions and quote for parts and labour. You’re under no obligation at all to have any extra work done by us.

Interim Service - 6 months service

This 6 month, 40+ point service is designed for the high mileage car user, or where the manufacturer recommends more frequent checks between annual services. This service includes an oil and filter change, top ups of essential fluids, as well as brake, steering and suspension checks (by removing the wheels).

Full Service - 12 months service

A full service will usually include everything that’s in the interim service plus extra checks and changes. It could include up to 70+ checks on your car. A full service includes, fuel filter replacement (for diesel cars), alternator hose and vacuum hose inspection, top up of brake fluid, power steering fluid and coolant.

The time taken to complete a service will depend on which service is booked. But as a rough guide:

  • An interim service is less extensive and includes fewer checks on your car. It may take around an hour or 2 hours.
  • A full service is a check of the whole car, so will take longer. It may take around 3 hours or longer.

Our mechanics will contact you when your car is ready to collect or provide you with a quote if any further work needs carrying out.

We have a fixed price for our services which does not include additional labour and parts if they need to be replaced.

  • Interim service - €125
  • Full service - €180

A service history is also something to look out for when you’re buying a used car. It shows you that the car’s been maintained. A missing service history could mean there’s a problem in the car’s past.

Making sure your car has a complete service history can help it to keep its value when you sell it. It’ll show buyers that it’s been well looked after and could help you get a better price for it.
